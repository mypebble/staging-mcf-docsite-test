Volunteers

Tip: You can view screens on this page at full size by clicking on the thumbnail image.

The Gift of Time
You will find that some people like to donate their time in addition to donating money, or just are more comfortable with or only able to contribute to your project through volunteering. People who have a desire to help by giving their time are invaluable assets to your income generation journey. Not only can they be called upon to perform labour-intensive tasks for your project, they are likely to be passionate about your project and are likely to spread the word, achieving a personal outreach that far exceeds traditional methods.

The Volunteers module is where you can manage your relationships with your network of volunteers, so you always have people to call upon to help you communicate your project needs to the wider community.

In the Volunteers module, you can search across your volunteers for those who are willing to do help out and for how much time they have pledged. For example, if you want people to hand out flyers for a particular project or to assist in other ways with the project, the Volunteers module will show you who is available for you to call upon.

You can build your volunteer network the following ways:

  • By running Campaign Messages.
  • Through your Joinos Community page where people can register an interest in volunteering to help you.
  • Word-of-mouth requests via other volunteers.

The Volunteering module links to the Campaigns module.

How do I build a volunteer network?

When you first begin to use this module it will be empty and you need to build your volunteer network. You can build your network by creating a campaign to contact your existing stakeholders in Campaigns to invite them to volunteer to support your projects and by adding volunteers manually.

Important! If you are adding volunteers who are going to work with children or adults, you need to ensure that the volunteer has passed the relevant DBS (previously CRB) checks. You will need to check this information outside Arro software in the usual way.

Running a campaign the first time

  1. Open Volunteers.
  2. Follow the prompt and then click Create Campaign to generate a campaign to contact your existing stakeholders in Campaigns to invite them to volunteer to support your projects.
  3. Follow the instructions in the Create Campaign wizard.

Running subsequent campaigns

Note: You need to have some active volunteers in order to run subsequent campaigns.

  1. Open Volunteers.
  2. Click Create Volunteer Campaign.
  3. Follow the prompt to enter the text that you want to send in an email to your active volunteer network.

Managing your volunteers

When you have some volunteers signed up, and you access the Volunteers module, you get an overview of your Volunteers in a dashboard.

The dashboard shows:

Volunteer Leads

 

This column shows the number of leads which have been added through your Community page or added manually to the Volunteers module.

If leads are not already in your Campaigns module, these details are automatically saved into the Campaigns module.

Active Volunteers

 

Once volunteers are accepted and approved, they appear in the list of Active Volunteers. This list shows the contact details of the volunteer and the project they have chosen to help with and time pledged per week..

Adding new volunteers

Leads are added automatically when the volunteer enters their details on the Volunteers form on your Joinos Community Page. You can add volunteer records in the Volunteers module manually - adding their details directly or selecting a contact from the Campaigns module. You can also add volunteers from the Campaigns module by selecting the Volunteer Status check box for the contact within the Campaigns module.

Note: There is space for notes about the volunteers you add. If the volunteer is required for specific purposes that involves working directly with children, you should ensure that the volunteer has passed DBS (previously CRB) checks and record this in the notes for the volunteer. You can also use the Notes section to record other qualifications the volunteer has, for example, current qualification in first aid training has qualified first aid training. For more information, see Adding Notes.

Getting volunteers through your community page

  • Ensure your Joinos Community page is published as the volunteer form is available for selection on this page.

    • The volunteer form collects the Name, Telephone No, Email address and a message, if one is included, from the volunteer and automatically creates a volunteer record in the Volunteers module.

    When a volunteer is added automatically, you receive an email notification of the volunteer’s interest and the volunteer receives on-screen confirmation that their submission has been sent. You will find the volunteer name listed in the Volunteer Leads section on the Volunteers dashboard.

Manually from within the Volunteers module

  • Ensure that you have all the volunteer lead forms collected if you are entering these details manually.
  1. Open Volunteers and click +Add New.
  • If you want to add a contact from Campaigns:
    1. Click + Contact.
    2. Search and select the Campaigns contact you want to add as a volunteer.

      3. Once you select the contact, the contact Name, Telephone Number and Email are automatically inserted into the Add Volunteer form.

  • If you want to add contact details manually:
    1. Enter the Name, Telephone Number and Email.
    2. Click Add Volunteer.
  1. If you have enough information to confirm the volunteer, you can do this now. For more information, see Confirming Volunteers.

Manually from within Campaigns

You can add contacts as volunteers by selecting the Volunteer Status check box for the contact within the Campaigns module. For more information, see Create a stakeholder.

Confirming Volunteers

  1. Open Volunteers.
  2. Click the lead card in the Volunteer Leads column.
  3. Select Contacted.
  4. Select the project the volunteer is going to help with from the To Volunteer on drop-down list.
  5. Enter the estimated amount of time the volunteer has pledged, selecting if this is in hours, days etc from the Estimate Time Pledge drop-down list.
  6. Click Confirm Volunteer.

Adding notes

At any time you can add notes for a volunteer lead. This gives you the ability to record the history of the contact you have with the volunteer.

Note: Add in this section, any notes applicable, for example, if the volunteer is going to work with children, has the volunteer passed the DBS (previously CRB) checks, or if they are trained in first aid when was their qualification date and last refresher date.

  1. Open Volunteers.
  2. Click the lead card in the Volunteer Leads column.
  3. Enter notes in the Contact History box.
  4. Click Post.

Removing volunteers

If a volunteer notifies you that they are no longer able to volunteer you can remove their details from the list of active volunteers by clearing the Volunteer Status check box for the contact in the Campaigns module. For more information, see Create a stakeholder.
