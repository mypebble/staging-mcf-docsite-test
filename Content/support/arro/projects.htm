The Importance of Projects
In order to achieve your vision you will need to run projects that deliver on the goals that support it. Projects almost always require financial support which you will raise through activities. You should always consider the sustainability of your projects before undertaking them. Supporters will want to see evidence of sustainability and you may need to plan to run further activities that raise finances for maintenance and repairs in future.

Projects are the fuel to your school or organisational objectives. So if one of your school objectives is to provide both high quality and a diverse set of sports, then one of your projects may be redeveloping your sports hall to cater for more than just football. Applying for a grant for your sports facility, for example, may be an activity you undertake to accomplish your project.

You can create community pages for your projects. These help you to promote your project and also act as a focal point for donations of time and money towards the project. Once you have created the project community page, you can promote it by sharing the URL to the page and creating campaigns to promote it. If you have purchased the Development Service, you can download automatically created flyers to send out to parents and businesses in the community about your project. You can share the project directly on Twitter, Facebook and LinkedIn, using the sharing buttons, just as if you were sharing a news article on social media.

Create a project

  1. Open Projects.
    • If you haven’t yet created a project, click Let’s create your first Project to get started.
    • If you have created one or more projects already, click View your Projects to display the projects dashboard.

      • The dashboard shows a list of all projects which have been created already and if they have been reviewed and approved by Pebble’s copywriter will show Pebble Approved alongside. Eventually all of your projects will show this.

      • To add a new project, click + New Project.

  2. To create a new project, you can:
    • Base the new project on an existing template from the project library.
    • Create the project, using a blank project template.

Base a new project on an existing project template

  1. Search the project library using the filters at the top to find project templates by Category, Cost, Impact or Timescale, or by scrolling through the pages of templates, using the number keys at the foot of the page .
  2. Click View Template to read the template details.
  3. To use this template as the basis for your project, click Choose this Template.

    4. The new project is built and the project details are displayed.

  4. To edit the newly constructed project, follow the instructions in Manage a project.

Create a project, using a blank project template

  1. Click Create Custom to display the Create Project screen.
  2. Enter the Project Title.

    3. Tip: Choose a title which is short and to the point but memorable and will grab attention.

  3. Enter your Project Description.

    4. This is where you describe the project as succinctly as you can. Provide information about what you are aiming to achieve with this project and what impact it will have. Don’t worry about including everything in the description, as you can expand on your points in the further sections. Try to make a big but positive impact with the description. It should tell the story of what you are trying to achieve, emphasising the gains the school and the local community will make through the project and should encapsulate the core of the project so that you can use it as an elevator pitch when people in local businesses or other interested parties ask you about your project.

    Tip: Click on Hints & Tips for additional help writing your description. Click on Hints & Tips again to close this information box.

  4. Enter a Start Date and, if you have a completion date in mind, enter this date also. If you don’t and the project is open-ended, select the check box, No Completion date.
  5. Enter the amount you estimate the project will cost in the How much will this project cost? box.
  6. Click Create Project.

    7. The project is created and the Project Details are displayed.

    You can either continue to enter these now or come back and complete the details at another time. For information on how to enter more information for your project, see Manage a project.

Manage a project

Within the Project Details screen, you can keep track of the finer details of your ongoing projects.

At the top of the screen you can monitor the project statistics: The project name, start date, completion date and strength are shown at the left hand side. The project funding target is shown at the right hand side.

Separate tabs on the screen allow you to add details to your project narrative, attach grants and other files to the project and also see any comments that have been left by colleagues if you shared the project details with them.

Edit the high level project details

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the list of Active Projects displayed.

  2. In the top section under the Funding Target amount, click Edit.
  3. Amend the project Name, Start date, Completion date, description, or Target as required.
  4. Click Save Project.

Edit the project narrative

You should include as much information as possible in your project narrative. Questions are provided for you to guide you through the detail of what you need to provide.

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the list of Active Projects displayed.

    2. The Narrative tab is automatically displayed first. This is the tab that needs to be displayed in order for you to enter further project information.

  2. Within the Narrative tab, click Edit.

    3. Text boxes appear on screen for you to answer the questions posed about your project. A Show Comments button appears which you can use to display comments from other users if you have shared the project with colleagues. For information on sharing the project, see Share the project internally with colleagues.

    Note: Guidance is given on the number of words you might like to enter for each section. However, the boxes expand to include all the text you enter.

  3. Enter your answers to the questions.

    4. Guidance in answering the questions is given below, following a simple example of wanting to fund iPads for use in a school.

    Tip: You can click Hints & Tips on each section to get further information on what to enter in each. Remember to click Hints & Tips again to close the Hints & Tips box when you have finished with it.

    Why do you need your project?

    		 

    Although the question is posed with a why question, your answer should really be a succinct what and why. Your answer must encompass the entire reason for the project and so adequately explain to your donors the reasons for the associated fundraising. You will need to research or have an idea of who the potential stakeholders in your project will be in order to appeal directly to them within this answer.

    For example, you want iPads available in classrooms to engage your pupils in specific interactive learning techniques available through iPad use and address the underachievement of pupils in maths or science. But who are your stakeholders? They may be pupils’ parents, even pupils themselves if they have a desire to learn a particular skill. They may be a local organisation driving for specific mathematical or scientific skills to be taught in local schools in order to harvest those later on in apprenticeships.
    You need to appeal to all of your potential donors when you write your narrative. For example, you want to invest in iPads in order to increase the employability of your pupils by teaching specific skills which they can learn through interactive iPad use; and also provide resources to the wider community.

    What impact will your project have?

    		 

    This is determined by the what, why and who (stakeholders).

    For example, if you are buying iPads to teach specific skills, this can benefit all other pupils who may have access to use iPads in school for other lessons. It will also benefit the organisations who are sponsoring the project and it can also benefit the wider community if you offer evening classes to the general public and make use of the iPads in these classes too.

    What supporting evidence do you have?

    		 

    Source any national resources to support your project, for example, there may be research to support increase in morale and well-being when pupils are offered teaching on the most up-to-date technologies. You can also include research you have conducted in school or research conducted in other local schools.

    For example, if another school has successfully used iPads, you could use their outcomes as evidence of impact.

    How does your project link to your school vision and goals?

    		 

    Link the supporting evidence you have cited to support the project back to your vision for your school.

    For example, your vision might be to be the school which offers the most innovative learning within IT within your region.

    How do you plan to deliver, monitor and evaluate your project?

    		 

    You need to plan how you will deliver your project and also how you will implement it.

    For example, if you are raising money to buy iPads, plan how you will raise that money and then how you will implement new teaching using the iPads.

    How many classes a week in your own school will be affected? Will you open up the facility to offer teaching to the wider community in evening classes? Will you let other local schools use the facility within your own school?

    How will you evaluate the project progress? Will you perform surveys before and after implementation to check the engagement of your own pupils and the wider community?

    How will you measure and report success?

    		 

    You have to be able to account for the result of spending money raised for the project.

    If you were raising money for iPads to teach a specific skill, you would be able to measure the result by the number of people taking up this particular lesson, perhaps even offering the service of additional teaching to the wider community of this particular lesson and measuring the uptake of that too.

    You can measure results in any of the following ways: issuing questionnaires, performing interviews, sending out parent surveys, recording a register of uptake and interest in the facility offered by the project. In terms of reporting success, you can publish a report on the school website or distribute to the wider community through local newspapers, libraries and other interested organisations.
  4. To save your answers to the questions, click Done.

Attach grants to a project

You can add grants to the project from within the Projects module or directly, from within the Grants module..

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the list of Active Projects displayed.

    2. The Narrative tab is automatically displayed.

  2. Select the Grants tab. Any grants that have already been added to the project are listed.

  3. To add grants to the project, click Search for Grants.

    4. This takes you to the Grants module where you can see all the grants that are available.

    You can drill down to find grants by project type (e.g. to find grants for languages or for physical education), by Grant Type, by Difficulty (the amount of detail required and the amount of time it will take to complete your application) or by Status (e.g. new, updated or expiring grants).

    You can display a list of grants you have saved by clicking the grey Star icon .

    Note: When you click this to select it, the colour changes from grey to yellow. You can clear the selection and display all grants again, by clicking the icon again. It will change from yellow to grey and all the grants will be displayed again.

    Or, if you know the name of the grant, you can use the Search box to enter the grant name .

    You can also use the Search box to look for grants that support specific things. For example, if you need to find a grant that relates to bereavement, type bereavement in the Search box and click Search.

  4. When you have found the grant you want click the Grant Title to open and view an outline of the award. To find out if you are eligible for the grant and if it is applicable for your project, read the full grant description by clicking Read More.

  5. To add the grant to the project, click + Add to Project.

Attach files to a project

You can add files to the project using the Files tab. This lets you keep all of your project information in one place.

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the list of Active Projects displayed. The Narrative tab is automatically displayed.
  2. Select the Files tab. Any files that have already been added to the project are listed here.
  3. To add files to the project, click + Add New. The Add File window opens.

    1. Click Choose a file, browse to select the file that you want to upload and click Open.
    2. Browse to select the file that you want to upload.
    3. Enter a memorable name for the file you are uploading. This makes it easy to identify when you read it in the list of files attached to the project.
    4. Click Upload.
      5. Is the file taking a long time to upload?

      There may be browser issues when uploading files to projects in IE browsers. If you find an upload is taking a long time then try uploading using Chrome or Firefox to see if this fixes the issue.

Share the project internally with colleagues

You can share the project with colleagues who can help you review the details and give you feedback on it.

Note: This is different from sharing the project on social media. When you are ready to do that, create the project community page and follow the instructions below on Promoting your project’s community page.

For example

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the list of Active Projects displayed. The Narrative tab is automatically displayed.
  2. Click Share with Colleague.

  3. Enter the Name and Email Address of the person you want to share the project details with and click Add Contact.
  4. Include a message to your colleague, for example, Please review and give feedback on the project details.

  5. Click Share Project.

    An email is sent to the contacts you have chosen to share the project with. The email contains a link which they can click which takes them to a page where they can add their comments.

View project comments

If you have shared a project with colleagues you will be able to view any feedback they have given you on the Comments tab and respond to their feedback. As comments and replies accrue, a history of the feedback is built up which you can refer back to.

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the list of Active Projects displayed. The Project tab is automatically displayed.
  2. Select the Comments tab. Any comments that have been received are automatically listed with the name of the contributor and the length of time since the comment was left.
  3. To reply to a comment, enter your reply in the Reply To Your Contributors box and click Add Reply.The reply is added to the list of comments and an email is sent to the contributor to notify them you have replied.

Create and edit the community page for your project

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the list of Active Projects displayed. The Narrative tab is automatically displayed.
  2. Create the community page for the first time or edit the existing community page.
    • To create a community page for the first time, click Create a Community Page at the top right-hand of the screen.

      • A community page is automatically created for you with the project details you have already entered.

    • To edit a community page that has already been created, click Edit Page.
  3. To edit any of the details on the community page, click Edit.
    • You can:
      • Change the title of the community page.
      • Change the text within the section Our Project Story.

        • You can add a link to other related web pages that relate to the project by wrapping your link in square brackets and inserting the link in normal brackets immediately afterwards. For example, [Click on this link](www.example.com). This will display the text Click on this link as a link in the published page and when you click on it, the web page www.example.com will open. You can open this in a new window or tab if you right-click on the link and select Open in a new window or Open in a new tab.

        Note: www.example.com is a fictitious URL, used for example purposes only.

      • Change the image by clicking Change Image and selecting an image from the drop-down menu of images.
      • Change the Key benefits of the project.
    • When you are finished editing, click I’m Done Editing or if you want to discard your changes, click Discard Changes.
  4. To publish the community page, click Publish.

    5. The community page URL link is created the first time you publish.This URL remains the same for the community page thereafter and when you publish updates these overwrite the information that was there before.

    You can copy and share the community page link on your school website, on the school Facebook page, through the school Twitter account, through emailing your contacts and also through individuals.

Promote your project’s community page

You need to promote your community page in order to make it work for your project. We offer different ways for you to do this.

Copy and share the link to your community page

As soon as you publish the community page, you’ll see two options: Copy link and Create a campaign.

  • To share the link to your community page, click Copy Link.

    • You can share the link to your community page on the school website, on Facebook, Twitter or LinkedIn.

    You can also encourage people to share your project by using the Facebook and Twitter buttons on your community page.

  • To create a campaign, click Create a campaign.

Copy published project link

Once you have published your community page, you can copy the link at any time in the following way:

  1. Open Projects.
  2. Select the Project.
  3. At the top of the project page, click Copy Link.
